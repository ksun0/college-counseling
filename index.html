Background

I graduated from Tsinghua University, Beijing, China with a CS and Economics dual degree. After getting a CS master's degree in the US, I started a career in the technology industry, and worked in companies such as Cisco, Amazon, and a few startups. I moved to Lexington, MA, when my kids attended middle schools there. Even though both my kids went to private high schools, I am pretty familiar with students' growth path and available resources in public schools as well.

I have two boys. My older one is the 2013 Mathcounts national champion. He graduated from Phillips Exeter Academy in 2017, and is one of the few high school students who went to three US Olympiad Camps (Math, Physics and USACO)。 He also did math and physics research outside of school, which won awards like Siemens Competition and Regeneron Science Talent Search finalist. He is one of the few students who is heavily involved in both STEM competitions and research. He graduated from Harvard in 2021 with a math bachelor and CS master double degree. My younger one is passionate about CS and Entrepreneurship (founder of HackNEHS). He figured this out after many other unsuccessful attempts, such as various math and science competitions. These experience was very frustrating, but was an unavoidable journey of self-exploration. It is good he finally found his true passion. He graduated from Phillips Andover in 2018, and graduated from UPenn in 2022 with double degress from Engineering College and Wharton Business School(M&T program), and a master degree in data stats.

During those years overseeing my two boys and other students, I accumulated a rich inventory of resources in high school competitions (math, physics, chemistry, biology, cs, writing, economics) and research (outside school and over the summer), as well as high quality program choices in business and entrepreneurship. In recent years, I also advised a few students interested in writing, debate and politics. Every student is unique, there is no way to clone any other person's activities. Blindly copying the growth path of other high achievers will only lead to more nervousness and frustration. Parents should encourage their kids to keep exploring one's own interests and potential, have a realistic realization of your capability and strength, and create a unique brand which belongs to yourself. Gradually learn the skills of critical thinking and decision making. Even though the planning is done to prepare and get ready for college applications, the ultimate goal is to train students into an independent, confident and impactful self. The two goals do not conflict.

I do not pick on student grades, but I pick on student attitude -- I am eager to work with self-disciplined students and reasonable/realistic parents. I am confident I can help students challenge themselves as long as we both work together with trust and strategies.

High School Service

I want to work with self-disciplined and motivated high school students to reach their personal goals, regardless their academic level. I help students with following items:

1. curriculum planning for the whole high school years, including course selection per semester or per term，and standard tests (AP, SAT, ACT, etc). For students who need help for school work, I have partner tutors in all subjects.
2. based on student interest and strength, recommend a few selective clubs or extracurricular activities, adjust direction accordingly as we go.
3. For advanced students, recommend out-of-school academic resources such as competition programs or research mentors, and prepare students with math, science, writing, research, entrepreneur, or business competitions.
4. recommend summer programs, help with application essay and communication with research mentors
5. pick a personal project to work on for longer term, which fits the student skill set while having a community impact. This is a critical item that adds strength to college application。
6. work with student on an execution plan, sometimes leverage my personal connections. When facing obstacles, discuss with students how to make a choice, evaluate pros and cons, help students develop critical thinking and independent problem solving skill sets. This service item is something I am very proud of. A few students in the past had said they benefited greatly while working with me.

The following two packages are available：
1. Advisor Role
Meet with student once a month, help parents to provide guidance to student. Identify areas to improve from my own observations. Does not include item 6 above。
Parents who fit this package: know their kid well and have time to guide their kid, but are not familiar with resources that they can make use of; need help crafting possible growth journeys。
Students who fit this pacakge：mature, good time management, self-motivated, can execute well based on instructions.

2. Parent Proxy
Guide students with all of their academics and activities, just like my own kid, try to explore their best potentials. Meet with students as frequently as once per week while school is in session.
Parents who fit this package：do not have time to guide their kids or have language barriers.
Students who fit this pacakge：need someone to monitor their academics closely and help with executions.

College Application

My package includes the following items:

1. Recommend a couple research oriented summer programs after 11th grade, try to correlate the program with student interest and college major. Help with application materials and application essays.
2. Recommend a list of colleges to apply based on students interests and strength. Around 15 schools, vary in difficulties.
3 Make a school visiting plan, and application milestones for the summer.
4. Review Common App applications, including 5 awards, 10 activities, essays, optional uploads, showing a clear persona in the overall portfolio (UC schools, MIT, Georgetown don't use Common App, Oxford/Cambridge has its own system too. They are included if students choose to apply.)
5. Brainstorming essays topics for all schools, maximized topic re-use
6. Assigned assay tutor will be responsible for polishing up to 10 school essays, UC system counts as one. (based on past experience, students can easily recycle essays for rest of schools beyond the 10th one). Essay is revised many times, until the tutor, student and me are all happy. Important essays will be reviewed by more than one tutor.
7. Advise which teacher to use for recommendation letters and strategies to ask for a quality letter.
8. Review parent and student questionnaires which influence recommendation letters
9. Answer all questions from students and parents during the application cycle. Turn around time is usually less than 24 hours.
10. Advise how to pick optional materials, such as resume, video upload, research abstract, etc.
11. Help students to talk to other students who are already in college to get insider information for a particular school.
12. Prepare for interview content and rehearsal interview
13. Advise off waiting list actions

My college advising has the following strength:
1. I have a very strong essay tutor team. These experienced tutors are selected over the past few years who can give concret suggestions and detailed instructions that students can follow. Every year I have students who came to me seeking essay help because they couldn't continue with their previous essay tutor.
2. I spent a lot time with studnts on how to ask for recommendation letters. Most college advisors let students do this on their own. I have a set of strategies to help students influence the content of their teacher recommendation letters, which directely contribute to better chance of admission. The key is to prepare early。
